Introduction of ICT

  • ICT stands for Information And Communication Technology.
  • It refers to the use of different telecommunications mediums such as the internet, cellphone, wi-fi, etc. to generate, access, transmit and manipulate the information.

Digital Society

  • A digital society is a society or community that is influenced and shaped by the widespread use of digital technologies. Such as computers, smartphones, internet, different social media.
  • In digital society, daily life education, work, etc. are directly influenced by the use of information and communication technology.

  • The key aspect of the digital society include:-

    1. Digital Connectivity

      • Digital Society is characterized by the high speed internet connectivity, people have access to digital communication tools including smartphones, computers, tablets, etc. for the online interaction.

    2. Digital Education

      • The education system in digital societies includes different technologies for learning and teaching technologies for learning and teaching process. This includes online education platforms like E-books, Virtual classrooms, etc.

    3. Digital Economy

      • Economics activities in digital society are heavily influenced by technology. E-commerce, Digital payment, Remote works are common in digital society.

    4. E-Health

      • E-Health refers to the use of digital technology and the internet to improve and support health and healthcare services. It encompasses electronic health records, telemedicine, Mobile health apps, and more.

    5. E-Governance

      • E-governance involves the use of information and communication (ICT) to deliver government services & Engage Citizens. It aims to make governmental processes more efficient, transparent and accessible.
      • This can include online portals for public services, digital voting systems & electronic communication between government & citizens.
      • So to it a digital society there is the use of E-governance.

Computer Ethics

  • As we know Ethics refers to the set of moral principles and rules that helps us to distinguish what is right and what is wrong.
  • Computer Ethics is the branch of ethics that is concerned with the moral and social issues that arise due to the use of computer and information technology.

  • The different fields that covers the Computer Ethics are as follows:

    1. Privacy

      • It addresses the issues related to the collection, storage and use of personal information such as unauthorized access to data, data breaches and handiving of sensitive information.

    2. Social Issues

      • Computer Ethics Explores the impact of technology on society such as job displacement, the digital divide and use of AI.

    3. Intellectual Property

      • It deals with how we can respect and protect the ownership and authorship of a digital content such as music, software, video, etc.

    4. Security

      • Computer Security involves the confidentiality, integrity and availability of data. Computer Ethics tells us how we can secure these data.

    5. Digital Citizenship

      • Computer Ethics also tells us how to promote the respectful and responsible use of computers and IT in society. It tells us about the rights of digital citizens.

Information Security

  • It is also known as “Infosec” is the practice of protecting the sensitive information and information system from unauthorized access, modification and destruction.
  • As we know in network or Cyber Space there is a chance of cyber attacks, which may lead to loss and damage to our data or information.
  • So information security helps to create a security environment for computers and users.

  • The main principles of information security are as follows:

    1. Confidentiality

      • It ensures that information is only accessible to authorized persons, which means an individual or any other system can not use or process other information without authorization.

    2. Integrity

      • It refers to the accuracy and reliability of data by preventing unauthorized modification or tampering.

    3. Availability

      • It ensures that the information and information system are accessible and usable when needed to the user, company and organization.

    4. Non-Repudiation

      • It is the concept enforced that ensures the party can not deny the authenticity or origin of a message or transaction.
      • It acts as a proof of integrity and origin of data. It prevents the party from later denying its involvement. For example, a digital signature that verifies the identity of the sender and authenticity of the message.

    5. Authenticity

      • It is a critical aspect that ensures the quality of being real, genuine or authentic. It includes user authentication, digital signature, time stamp, etc.

Information Security Policy

  • The information security policy is a set of rules and guidlines that commands how IT assets and resources should be used, managed and protected.

  • Some of the key elements of INFOSEC Policy are:

    1. Define the objectives, scope and purpose of the policy.
    2. Assign the roles and responsibility on the policy owner.
    3. Provide clear and concise statements of the rules for information security.
    4. Defining standards and best practices for security of the organization network infrastructure.
    5. Outlines employed training and Awareness programs to mitigate the risk associated with the different threats.

Concept of Cybercrime

  • It refers to any criminal activities that involve the use of computers, network and digital devices.
  • These cybercrimes can target individuals, businesses, organisations, government, etc. and can cause various types of harm. Such as financial loss, data breaches, privacy violation, etc.

  • Some example of cybercrime includes:

    1. Hacking

      • This is the unauthorized access to the computer system or network to gain information, disturb operations, or cause damage.
      • While hacking can be used for ethical purposes such as identifying vulnerabilities in systems (known as ethical hacking or penetration testing) it's often associated with illegal activities.

    2. Online Scam

      • It refers to the illegal activities conducted on the internet to gain financial benefits or cause harm to individuals or organisations. Such as phishing, online shopping scam, investment scam, etc.

    3. Phishing

      • This is the attempt to trap users into revealing their personal and sensitive data and information such as password, credit card details, bank account details, etc. by sending them fraudulent E-mail or message that appears to be a form of real source.

    4. Malware

      • It is the general term for any software that is designed to harm and exploit another software. Malware can take various forms such as viruses, spyware, ransomware, trojan horse, etc. These software slow down your devices, display unwanted ads and pop-ups and steal your personal and financial information etc.

    5. DDOS Attack

      • It refers to the Distribution Denial of Service. It is a type of cyber attack that aims to make a website or service unavailable by overwhelming it with traffic from multiple sources.
      • A DOS(Denial of Service) is different from DDOS attack, in this type of attack only the source of traffic is used for attack.

    6. Cyberspionage

      • It refers to the use of digital techniques to gain unauthorized access to confidential information typically for political, economical or military purposes. It involves the theft of sensitive data such as government secrets, trade secrets and other valuable information.

Prevention of Cybercrime

  • Prevention of cybercrimes involves a combination of technical legal and Educational measures.

  • Here are some ways to prevent cybercrimes.

    1. Use Strong password and change them frequently

      • Use a combination of uppercase, lowercase, letters, numbers and special symbols in your password and avoid using easily guessable information like your name, date of birth as password.

    2. Keep software updated

      • Regularly update your OS, antivirus software and other security applications to ensure they have the latest security patches.

    3. Backup your data

      • Regularly backup your important data on an external hard drive or cloud storage service. So, you can easily restore it in case your drive is compromised or lost.

    4. Be careful when clicking links

      • Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown or suspicious sources as they may contain malware or phishing scams designed to steal your personal info.

    5. Educate yourself

      • Stay informed about the latest cyber threads by reading news articles or attending cybersecurity workshops or seminars.

    6. Report Suspicious activity

      • If you suspect any suspicious activity or receive any suspicious e-mails or messages, report it immediately.

Intellectual property rights

  • Intellectual property refers to the creation of minds such as invention, literacy, artistic work, design and symbols. These creations are protected by the law through various mechanisms.

  • Let explore the main type of intellectual property right.

    1. Patent rights:

      • It is the exclusive right granted to the inventor for a limited period of time (usually 20 years) to protect their new invention.

    2. Copyright

      • It protects creator’s rights over literacy and artistic works. It covers a wide range of creation from book, music to computer program, advertisement etc. copyright typically lasts for life of author plus 70 years.

    3. Trade Mark

      • The different symbols, names, logos, sign, etc. that help to distinguish the goods and services of one enterprise from another.
      • For eg: NIKE Logo is the trade mark that identifiers the companies and its products. It lasts for 10 years and is renewed after every 10 years.

    4. Industrial designed

      • Protect the visual design of objects such as shape, size, colour, etc.
      • For example: the coca-cola bottle is an industrial design that has a distinctive shape and appearance. It lasts for 15 years in the U.S. and 25 years in other countries.

    5. Geographical indication

      • It indicates that a product comes from a specific geographical area and possesses quality, reputation, and characteristic associated with that place. For eg: APPLE of mustang.

Concept of Digital Signature

  • The term digital signature is the cryptographic technique that is used to verify the authenticity and integrity of digital documents.
  • It is the electronic marks that make sure the documents.
  • When you digitally sign a document your software generates a hash(a fixed length of alphanumeric value) from the document content. The hash is then encrypted using your private key to create a digital signature. The receiver can check the signature by decrypting it with the public key and comparing the resulting hash with the original document.

  • Your digital signature reflects your identity just like your hand written signature. If you need to create one, you can use online tools like signaturely or signwell to draw or type your digital signature and apply it with your digital documents.

    Advantage of Digital Signature

    • It has various application across different domau or fields some of them are follows:
    • It is used for secure email communication.
    • Used for secure online transactions.
    • Used for identifying participants or senders in any online transaction.
    • Used in web applications and its API.
    • It is widely used in government service and applications such as application for tenders, E-filling of income tax and other relevant applications.

Concept of Cyber Law in Nepal

  • It is one of the laws that deals with internet related technology. It covers a wide range such as online fraud, intellectual property rights, harassment and other different cyber crimes.
  • It is important for protecting peoples, properties, their sensitive details and government from online crime and violation.
  • In the past the government authorities dealt with cybercrime by means of public offence act. These acts didn’t cover the technical aspects of cybercrimes so realizing these issues, the government promoted a cyber law called Electronic Transaction And Digital Signature Act(ETDSA) in 2006(B.S 2063) which is popularly known as Cyber law of Nepal.

  • Some of the major features of this law as follows:

    1. This law has provisions for punishing both individuals and utitutions for voting cyber law.
    2. This law also makes online transactions more secure.
    3. It addresses issues related to intellectual property, including patents, trademarks and copyrights.
    4. The law prohibits unauthorized online gambling activities. It makes strict regulations for online gaming and betting platforms.
    5. This law also addresses online defamation, which means saying or writing something false about someone that hurts their reputation. It can make others think badivy of the person which can lead to legal consequences like fines or having to pay for damages.

ICT Policy in Nepal

  • It refers to the set of rules, guidlines and principles that are used to govern the use, management and implementation of ICT within an organisation, government or society.

  • To address the different issues in ICT sectors, the ICT policy 2015 was implemented whose main vision was transforming Nepal to information and knowledge based society through the use of ICT.

    Major goals of ICT Policy in Nepal

    1. Place Nepal in the top second quartile in Electronic Government Development Index(EGDI) by the end of 2020.
    2. At least 75% of the population will have digital literacy.
    3. 90% of people have broadband(High speed internet) access.
    4. The contribution of the ICT sector(except telecom) in GDP will cross 25%.
    5. Everyone will have internet access.
    6. 80% of the government service will be provided through online.
